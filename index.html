What is Ragging?

Ragging refers to any act of physical or mental abuse, harassment, or humiliation imposed by senior students on their juniors, particularly in educational institutions. Originally perceived as a form of initiation, ragging has evolved into a practice that often crosses the line into bullying, causing severe distress to victims. It can range from seemingly harmless tasks to extreme forms of abuse, including verbal, physical, and psychological torment.

Laws & Regulations

The Supreme Court of India’s Directives (2009):

  • In response to increasing incidents of ragging, the Supreme Court of India issued directives that made ragging a criminal offense.
  • Institutions are required to take immediate action against those involved in ragging, including filing FIRs (First Information Reports) with the police.
  • Students found guilty of ragging may face suspension, expulsion, and even imprisonment.

The University Grants Commission (UGC) Regulations on Curbing the Menace of Ragging in Higher Educational Institutions (2009):

  • The UGC mandates that every university and college must form an Anti-Ragging Committee and Anti-Ragging Squad.
  • Institutions are required to include a clause prohibiting ragging in their admission forms and prospectus
  • The UGC provides a toll-free anti-ragging helpline for students to report incidents.

The Indian Penal Code (IPC):

  • Sections 294, 323, 324, 325, 326, 339, 340, 341, 342, and 506 of the IPC can be invoked depending on the severity of the ragging incident.
  • These sections cover a range of punishable offenses, from causing harm and wrongful restraint to criminal intimidation.

University Anti-Ragging Policy:

Most universities have their own anti-ragging policies that align with national laws. These policies typically include:


  • A detailed procedure for filing complaints.
  • Strict disciplinary actions against perpetrators, including rustication or expulsion.
  • Mandatory orientation sessions for new students and their guardians on the issue of ragging.
